ClawBuddy Commercial Script

Working title: “The Button That Gets It”
Format: 90-second parody launch ad + 30-second cutdown
Tone: clean tech-launch spoof, warm, useful, self-aware — not mean, not a direct copy.
ClawBuddy: OpenClaw in your pocket. One button. Real help. No keynote fog machine required.
Creative direction: This is an original parody of the hyper-polished AI hardware reveal genre. It borrows the mood — dramatic pauses, overconfident product language, “future is now” staging — without copying lines, scenes, or exact structure from the referenced ad.

90-Second Spot

TimeVisualAudio / Dialogue
0:00–0:06 Black screen. A tiny green glow pulses.
Slow macro shot of ClawBuddy on a desk like it is a sacred artifact.		 VO, very serious:
For years, humanity has asked one question:
“What if my assistant was not trapped in seventeen browser tabs?”
0:07–0:14 Cut to a person staring at a laptop with 42 tabs, six calendars, and one spreadsheet named “final_FINAL_use_this_one_v9.xlsx”. Person, whispering: I just need the thing… from the other thing.
VO: We built a device for that deeply modern crisis.
0:15–0:23 Hero shot. Hand picks up ClawBuddy. The screen wakes with a simple ClawBuddy mark. VO: Meet ClawBuddy. A portable OpenClaw companion that listens when you ask, thinks with your agent, and answers without making you open a laptop like it’s tax season.
0:24–0:32 Finger presses and holds the button. Instant listening animation appears. Person: ClawBuddy, remind me what I was doing before I got distracted by making this commercial.
ClawBuddy: You were applying to jobs, fixing a dashboard, and somehow launching a hardware company before lunch.
0:33–0:42 Quick montage: ClawBuddy on desk, in car cup holder, clipped near a workbench, next to a coffee mug. Text overlays: “Push to talk.” “Ask your agent.” “Get back to work.” VO: No app maze. No fake productivity ritual. Push. Talk. Let your actual agent do the useful part.
0:43–0:52 Shot of “setup” screen. Friendly labels: Wi‑Fi, OTA URL, Pair Device. A checkmark appears: “Paired.” VO: Pair it once. Keep the private stuff private. Unpaired devices get politely ignored — the way all suspicious little computers should be.
0:53–1:03 Person holds ClawBuddy near a pile of cables and a tiny ESP board. Optional camera icon appears. Person: What am I looking at?
ClawBuddy: A project that started as “quick test” and became a lifestyle. Also, the black cable is probably the data cable.
1:04–1:14 Overly dramatic product spin, parody keynote lighting. Screen says “OpenClaw on the go.” VO: It can help with reminders, notes, status checks, job-search chaos, smart-home tasks, project updates, and the sacred ritual of asking, “wait, did we already fix that?”
1:15–1:24 Person walking away from laptop, finally calm. ClawBuddy sits beside keys/wallet. Person: ClawBuddy, what’s next?
ClawBuddy: I already queued it up. Also, drink water. You’ve had coffee and vibes.
1:25–1:30 Logo lockup: ClawBuddy mark + wordmark. Brand colors: Claw Blue / Buddy Mint on Night Slate. VO: ClawBuddy.
One button between you and getting your life together.
Public prototype. Actual usefulness may cause additional projects.

30-Second Cutdown

TimeVisualAudio / Dialogue
0:00–0:04 Person buried in tabs/spreadsheets. VO: Your assistant should not require a laptop, three logins, and emotional resilience.
0:05–0:10 Hand picks up ClawBuddy. Button hold. Instant listening glow. VO: Meet ClawBuddy. OpenClaw on the go.
0:11–0:18 Fast montage: desk, garage, car, kitchen counter. Person: What’s next?
ClawBuddy: I checked. You already fixed it. The remaining problem is ambition.
0:19–0:25 Setup screen: Pair Device. OTA URL. Checkmark. VO: Pair it. Keep it private. Ask your agent from anywhere.
0:26–0:30 Logo lockup. VO: ClawBuddy. Push to talk. Built to help.

Shot List

  • Macro product hero: ClawBuddy screen glow on dark desk.
  • Human chaos: laptop tabs, messy notes, spreadsheet, coffee.
  • Instant PTT: thumb hold, clear listening state.
  • Anywhere utility montage: desk, car, garage/workbench, kitchen.
  • Pairing/setup screen: friendly “Paired” checkmark, no scary networking jargon.
  • Logo end card using Claw Blue, Buddy Mint, Night Slate.

Performance Notes

  • Voiceover should be calm, expensive, and slightly absurd.
  • ClawBuddy voice should be dry, helpful, and concise.
  • Do not over-explain technical setup in the ad. Show “Pair Device” and “Works anywhere” simply.
  • Keep parody broad: tech-launch seriousness, not a direct recreation of any copyrighted scene.

Optional Taglines

  • OpenClaw on the go.
  • One button between you and getting your life together.
  • Push to talk. Built to help.
  • Your agent, off the leash.
  • The tiny device for people with too many projects.

ClawBuddy
OpenClaw on the go.
